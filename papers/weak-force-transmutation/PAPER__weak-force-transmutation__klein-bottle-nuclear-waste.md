## Abstract {-}

The companion paper (Carpenter, 2026k) established that the Klein bottle eigenvalue $\beta^{-1} = 43.619\,053$ has floor 43 — the atomic number of technetium, the lightest element with no stable isotopes. Every technetium isotope decays exclusively by beta decay (weak force). This paper extends the result in two directions.

First, the weak force is the **only** fundamental interaction that changes one element into another. Strong, electromagnetic, and gravitational interactions rearrange, bind, repel, and curve — but none of them change $Z$. Only the weak force converts neutrons to protons (or vice versa), and thereby transmutes one element into a different element. Every long-lived fission product in the nuclear waste inventory resolves to a stable endpoint through the same mechanism: beta decay.

Second — and this is the deeper claim — technetium is not waste. It is the **nuclear-scale fixed point** of the constraint surface. The companion papers establish that molecular oxygen is maintained at a fixed-point concentration at every chemical and planetary scale by Le Chatelier feedback: perturbation produces a counter-response that opposes the perturbation (Carpenter, 2026e, 2026f, 2026i). This paper identifies the same architecture at the nuclear scale. Fission perturbs the system by releasing excess neutrons. The most abundant fission product at the mass-99 chain — technetium-99 — absorbs those neutrons ($\sigma = 22.8$ b), dampening the flux that would otherwise cause further fission and activation. The perturbation produces the product that opposes the perturbation. This is Le Chatelier at the nuclear scale.

What oxygen is to chemistry, technetium is to nuclear physics. The gap in the periodic table at $Z = 43$ is not a defect. It is the repair station — the point where the constraint surface processes radiation that the electromagnetic (Level 0) layer cannot filter. The same cross-scale invariance that holds from blood to wind to stellar convection holds from the oxygen fixed point to the technetium fixed point. What is true at one level is true at all levels.

## 1. The Transmutation Operator

### 1.1 Four Forces, One Transmuter

The four fundamental interactions of the Standard Model are:

| Force | Mediator | Range | Changes $Z$? | Changes $A$? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strong | Gluon | $\sim 10^{-15}$ m | No$^*$ | No (binds) |
| Electromagnetic | Photon | $\infty$ | No | No |
| Weak | $W^{\pm}$, $Z^0$ | $\sim 10^{-18}$ m | **Yes** | No |
| Gravitational | (Graviton) | $\infty$ | No | No |

$^*$Alpha decay changes $Z$ by 2 and $A$ by 4 via ejection of a pre-formed $^{4}$He cluster. Proton radioactivity (observed in extreme proton-drip-line nuclei such as $^{151}$Lu) changes $Z$ by 1 via tunnelling. Neither involves quark flavour change ($d \leftrightarrow u$). See Boundary #2.

Only the weak force changes $Z$ by **converting a nucleon's quark flavour** — the sole mechanism that transforms one element into another at the fundamental level. The mechanism is specific:

$$n \to p + e^{-} + \bar{\nu}_e \qquad (\beta^{-} \text{ decay: } Z \to Z+1)$$

$$p \to n + e^{+} + \nu_e \qquad (\beta^{+} \text{ decay: } Z \to Z-1)$$

$$p + e^{-} \to n + \nu_e \qquad (\text{electron capture: } Z \to Z-1)$$

These are the only processes in nature that convert one element into a different element at the nuclear level. Alpha decay ($\alpha$ emission) changes both $Z$ and $A$, but the alpha particle itself is a pre-formed $^{4}$He nucleus ejected by the strong force — the element change in the daughter is a bookkeeping consequence, not a quark-level flavour change. In contrast, beta decay involves a $W$ boson mediating quark flavour change ($d \to u$ or $u \to d$). This is a weak interaction. There is no strong-force or electromagnetic analogue.

**The weak force is the transmutation operator.** Alpha decay and proton emission rearrange or eject pre-existing protons; they do not convert neutrons to protons at the quark level. Only the weak force changes nucleon identity — and therefore changes element identity through flavour conversion.

### 1.2 Connection to the Klein Bottle Eigenvalue

The companion paper (Carpenter, 2026k) established:

$$\beta^{-1} + S \cdot \beta = 4\pi^2 + \pi + 1$$

with $\beta^{-1} = 43.619\,053$ and floor $= 43 =$ technetium. The Level 1 eigenvalue sits at the element whose every isotope is transmuted by the weak force. The eigenvalue floor is the atomic number of the element that is **defined** by the transmutation operator.

This paper's thesis: the connection is not accidental. The Klein bottle eigenvalue identifies the operator, not just the element. $Z = 43$ is where the operator is maximally expressed — no stable endpoint, no competing channel, pure beta decay. But the operator acts on every element that needs to change identity. Nuclear waste is the operator's workload.

The total waste problem — across all isotopes, all mass numbers, all reactors — reduces to: **how long until the weak force finishes?**

## 3. Technetium as the Floor

### 3.1 Why Technetium Is Special

Element 43 is distinguished from every other fission product by three properties:

1. **No stable isotopes.** Tc is not waiting to reach a stable isotope of technetium. There is no such thing. Every technetium isotope decays. The element itself is a transient — it exists only in transit. All other long-lived fission products ($^{137}$Cs, $^{90}$Sr, $^{129}$I) have stable isotopes of the same element. Caesium-133 is stable. Strontium-88 is stable. Iodine-127 is stable. The radioactive isotopes in the waste are unstable versions of otherwise stable elements. Technetium is different: it has no stable version. The element is defined by its instability.

2. **Pure beta decay.** Every technetium ground-state isotope decays by $\beta^{-}$, $\beta^{+}$, or electron capture. There are no alpha-decay channels, no spontaneous fission, no proton emission. Nuclear isomers (e.g., Tc-99m) may de-excite by isomeric transition (IT, electromagnetic), but IT does not change $Z$ — the resulting ground state still beta-decays. The decay mode column for element-changing transitions is uniform. Tc-99, the dominant waste isotope, undergoes $\beta^{-}$ decay to Ru-99 with $Q = 293.5$ keV — an unusually low Q-value, reflecting how close technetium comes to stability. But close is not enough. Beta decay always finds the channel.

3. **Dominant fission product.** At $\sim 20$ kg per GW-year of reactor operation, Tc-99 is one of the largest contributors to long-lived fission waste by mass. It is not a trace species. It is a primary output of fission.

These three properties — no stable isotopes, pure beta decay, high fission yield — make technetium the purest expression of the transmutation operator in the waste inventory. It is not merely one item on the list. It is the element that **is** the list: permanently unstable, purely weak-force mediated, produced in bulk by fission.

## 5. Transmutation Programs

### 5.1 Engineered Acceleration of the Weak Force

The natural timescale for Tc-99 to resolve is 211,100 years ($t_{1/2}$). This is too long for practical waste management. Transmutation programs aim to shorten it.

The dominant approach is **neutron bombardment**: expose Tc-99 to a high neutron flux. The neutron capture cross-section of Tc-99 is:

- Thermal $\sigma(n,\gamma) = 22.8 \pm 1.1$ b
- Resonance integral $= 340 \pm 20$ b

Neutron capture transforms $^{99}$Tc into $^{100}$Tc:

$$^{99}\text{Tc} + n \to {^{100}\text{Tc}} \xrightarrow{\beta^{-}} {^{100}\text{Ru}} \quad (t_{1/2} = 15.8 \text{ s})$$

The trick: $^{100}$Tc has a half-life of 15.8 seconds. The same weak-force beta decay that would have taken 211,100 years for $^{99}$Tc now completes in seconds for $^{100}$Tc. The strong force (neutron capture) rearranges the nucleus. The weak force (beta decay) still performs the transmutation. The element change is still and only $\beta^{-}$.

This is the structure of every transmutation scheme:
1. Use the strong force (neutron capture) or electromagnetic force (photon excitation) to rearrange the nucleus into an excited or neutron-rich state.
2. The rearranged nucleus then beta-decays (weak force) to a stable element.
3. The element change — the transmutation itself — is always the weak force.

The strong force and electromagnetic force are logistics. The weak force is the operator. You can speed up delivery to the operator's window, but you cannot replace the operator.

### 5.3 The Irreducibility

Could a different mechanism resolve nuclear waste?

- **Strong force alone?** Neutron capture without subsequent beta decay produces a heavier isotope of the same element. $^{99}$Tc $+ n \to$ $^{100}$Tc — still technetium, still no stable isotope available. Without beta decay, neutron capture on Tc just makes more unstable Tc isotopes.
- **Electromagnetic force?** Photodisintegration ($\gamma, n$) can strip neutrons, but the result is a lighter isotope of the same element. $^{99}$Tc $-n \to$ $^{98}$Tc — still technetium, still unstable, still requiring beta decay.
- **Fission?** Neutron-induced fission of fission products is energetically forbidden for the light fission products (too far from the fissile region). Only the heavy actinides can fission, and their fission produces... more fission products, which beta-decay.
- **Proton bombardment?** Spallation can smash nuclei apart, but the fragments are neutron-rich and beta-decay to stability.

Every path terminates at the weak force. The operator is irreducible. There is no alternative service window.

## 6. The Topological Argument

### 6.1 Non-Orientability as Cause and Resolution

The weak force violates parity (Wu et al., 1957). In the constraint surface framework (Carpenter, 2026k), parity violation corresponds to non-orientability — the Klein bottle layer of the eigenvalue tower.

The non-orientability has two consequences:

**As cause:** Parity violation enables decay channels that a parity-symmetric interaction would forbid. The V$-$A structure of the weak current couples exclusively to left-handed particles. This selectivity — this breaking of left-right symmetry — is what allows certain nuclear transitions that conserve angular momentum, parity, and isospin in a specific (parity-violating) way. Without parity violation, some beta-decay channels would be forbidden or suppressed. The weak force's non-orientable topology enables the very decays that make nuclei unstable.

**As resolution:** The same parity-violating coupling that opens the decay channel also executes the decay. The $W^{\pm}$ boson mediates the $d \to u$ (or $u \to d$) quark transition. The operator that creates the instability is the operator that resolves it. There is no separate "stabilisation force." The weak force destabilises and stabilises by the same mechanism — it just takes time.

In the collapse algebra:

- The admissible set $A$ is the set of states the system can occupy.

Nuclear waste isotopes occupy states within the Level 1 (Klein bottle) region of the constraint surface. Their current element identity ($Z$) is not their final element identity. The collapse operator at Level 1 is beta decay. It will act — the operator is total — but the timing is determined by the coupling strength at that level ($\mathcal{O}_1 = 2.19 \times 10^{-5}$, the Level 1 overshoot) and the specific nuclear matrix element of each isotope.

The constraint surface does not compute half-lives. It identifies the operator class. Every isotope in the Level 1 region resolves by the Level 1 operator. The operator is the weak force. The resolution is beta decay. The queue is the waste inventory.

In the eigenvalue tower:

The gap in the periodic table at $Z = 43$ has been treated as a curiosity — the lightest element with no stable isotopes. ### 8.2 What Would Falsify the Argument

1. **Discovery of a non-weak-force flavour-changing transmutation mechanism.** If a process were found that converts a neutron to a proton (or vice versa) within a nucleus without involving $W^{\pm}$ boson exchange, the claim that the weak force is the sole flavour-changing transmutation operator would be falsified. Alpha decay and proton emission change $Z$ but do not convert nucleon identity (see Boundary #2). No flavour-changing non-weak mechanism is known or theoretically predicted within the Standard Model.

2. **A stable technetium isotope.** If a technetium isotope were found to be stable (or with a half-life exceeding the age of the universe), the claim that the operator is total at $Z = 43$ would be weakened. Current nuclear theory and experiment exclude this: all mass numbers accessible to Tc are blocked by Mo ($Z = 42$) or Ru ($Z = 44$) via the Mattauch rule.

3. **Resolution of a fission product by a non-beta-decay pathway.** If a long-lived fission product were found to resolve to a stable endpoint without any beta-decay step in its chain, the universality claim would be falsified. Current nuclear data tables show no such case.

### 8.3 What the Argument Does Not Predict

The argument does not predict:
- Half-lives of specific isotopes.
- Neutron capture cross-sections.
- Nuclear matrix elements.
- The branching ratios of complex decay chains.
- The optimal design of transmutation reactors.

These are quantitative outputs of nuclear physics calculations involving the strong force (nuclear structure), electromagnetic force (Coulomb barriers, internal conversion), and weak force (beta-decay rates). The argument is structural, not computational. It identifies the operator. It does not compute the operator's output for specific inputs.

### 8.4 Accuracy of the Structural Claim

The structural claim has three components, each with a different epistemic status:

1. **"The weak force is the only force that changes element identity through nucleon conversion."** This is established Standard Model physics. ## Boundaries {-}

1. **The floor operation is not a derivation.** $\lfloor 43.619 \rfloor = 43$ is a structural identification, not a mathematical proof. The fractional part is unexplained. The argument rests on convergence of independent facts, not on algebraic necessity.

2. **Alpha decay and proton emission change $Z$ without flavour change.** Alpha decay reduces $Z$ by 2 via ejection of a pre-formed $^{4}$He cluster. Proton radioactivity (observed in extreme proton-drip-line nuclei such as $^{151}$Lu, $^{109}$I) reduces $Z$ by 1 via Coulomb tunnelling. Neither involves $W^{\pm}$ exchange or quark flavour change ($d \leftrightarrow u$). This paper's claim is precisely: **flavour-changing transmutation** — the conversion of a neutron to a proton or vice versa within a nucleus — is exclusively weak-force mediated. Alpha emission and proton emission rearrange or eject pre-existing protons; they do not convert nucleon identity. The distinction matters: in the fission waste inventory, every element-identity change in the long-lived fission products proceeds by beta decay, not by alpha emission or proton tunnelling. The structural argument is about the operator that resolves nuclear waste, and that operator is the weak force.

3. **The argument is structural, not quantitative.** No half-lives, cross-sections, or matrix elements are derived. The argument identifies the operator, not the operator's eigenvalues. A quantitative extension would require nuclear structure calculations that are beyond the scope of the constraint surface algebra.

4. **Promethium ($Z = 61$) shares the mechanism.** The second element with no stable isotopes below bismuth is promethium. It also decays exclusively by $\beta^{\pm}$ and EC. The eigenvalue tower does not predict $Z = 61$. A complete theory would need to account for both holes in the periodic table. The Klein bottle paper noted this; this paper inherits the limitation.

5. **The topological identification is interpretive.** Calling the weak force "the non-orientable layer" rests on Hadley (1997, 2000) and the structural arguments of the companion papers. This is not mainstream physics. The chain from Klein bottle to parity violation to weak force has a published-but-not-mainstream link (Hadley) that the argument depends on.

6. **Nuclear data uncertainties.** Cross-sections for transmutation targets (e.g., Tc-99) have 10–30% uncertainties, and nuclear data libraries (ENDF/B, JEFF, JENDL) disagree by 10–20% on resonance parameters. The structural argument is unaffected by these uncertainties — it does not use cross-section values. But any quantitative extension would inherit them.

7. **The Le Chatelier framing is structural, not quantitative.** This paper claims Tc-99 is a Le Chatelier product at the nuclear scale: perturbation (fission) produces counter-product (Tc-99 neutron absorption). This is qualitatively consistent with reactor physics — Tc-99 is a classified neutron poison. But the quantitative contribution of Tc-99 to neutron economy in a reactor is one of many fission-product poisons (Xe-135, Sm-149, and others dominate on short timescales). The Le Chatelier claim is about the structural role across all timescales, not about Tc-99 being the dominant short-term neutron absorber. On geological timescales — the Oklo timescale — Xe-135 and Sm-149 have long since reached equilibrium or decayed; Tc-99's 211,000-year presence is the long-term dampener.

8. **The cross-scale invariance is a pattern, not a proof.** The isomorphism between O₂-at-Level-0 and Tc-at-Level-1 is structurally compelling but not mathematically derived from the eigenvalue tower. The tower produces the number 43. The connection from 43 to "nuclear repair station" passes through nuclear physics, Le Chatelier reasoning, and the cross-scale architecture of the companion papers. Each link is independently supported, but the full chain is a synthesis, not a theorem.
