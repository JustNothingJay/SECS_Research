## Abstract {-}

The companion paper (Carpenter, 2026n) identified technetium ($Z = 43$) as the floor of the Klein bottle eigenvalue $\beta^{-1} = 43.619\,053$ — the nuclear-scale repair station where fission's perturbation produces its own dampener via Le Chatelier feedback. That paper carried a boundary statement: "The tower predicts $Z = 43$ but not $Z = 61$. A complete theory would need to account for both holes in the periodic table."

This paper resolves the boundary.

The Klein bottle is topologically two Möbius strips glued at their boundary. The diagonal through both layers scales the eigenvalue by $\sqrt{2}$:

$$\lfloor \beta^{-1} \sqrt{2} \rfloor = \lfloor 61.687 \rfloor = 61 = \text{Promethium}$$

Promethium ($Z = 61$), the second element with no stable isotopes below bismuth, is not an unexplained anomaly. It is the Klein bottle's diagonal eigenvalue. Like technetium, every promethium isotope decays exclusively by weak-force channels ($\beta^{-}$, EC). The mechanism is identical. The topology predicts both holes.

But the critical result is not promethium. It is what promethium **decays to**.

| Chain | Unstable processor | Product | Product's $\sigma_{n,\gamma}$ | Function |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tc chain** | Tc-99 ($t_{1/2} = 211{,}100$ yr) | Ru-99 | $\sim 5$ b | **Inert** — sits there, does nothing |
| **Pm chain** | Pm-149 ($t_{1/2} = 53.1$ hr) | **Sm-149** | **40,140 b** | **Shield** — absorbs 1,761× more than Tc-99 |

Technetium processes the perturbation and its product is inert. Promethium processes the perturbation and its product is **the most powerful stable neutron absorber among fission products**. The first hole makes the processor. The second hole makes the shield.

The golden ratio extends the sub-tower to its boundary:

$$\lfloor \beta^{-1} \sqrt{2} \cdot \varphi \rfloor = \lfloor 99.811 \rfloor = 99 \approx 100 = \text{Fermium}$$

Fermium ($Z = 100$) is where neutron capture terminates — beyond it, nuclei fission faster than neutrons can be added. The Level 1 constructive mechanism stops.

Three operators from one eigenvalue and two geometric constants:

| Scale factor | Value | Floor | Element | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\beta^{-1}$ | 43.619 | 43 | **Tc** | Queue processor |

This paper applies the three-operator structure to the specific problem of Chernobyl and Fukushima remediation. The constraint surface does not merely identify the problem. It identifies the solution architecture: **process** (Tc), **shield** (Sm), **bound** (Fm). The biological extension — how living systems metabolise Level 1 perturbations at Level 0 — completes the framework via the Chernobyl frog evidence and the mutation-metabolism doctrine.

## What This Paper Says, In Plain Language {-}

Chernobyl and Fukushima broke the container. The radioactive atoms that should have stayed inside the reactor — decaying slowly, safely, inside steel and concrete — were scattered across the landscape. They are now in the soil, the water, the plants, the animals. They are still decaying. They will finish decaying. But they are doing it in the open, surrounded by biology that was never designed to handle it.

The previous papers showed that the mathematics of the constraint surface — the fundamental equation that produces the fine structure constant — contains a hidden level whose floor lands at element 43, technetium. Technetium is the nuclear repair station: fission produces it, it absorbs the excess neutrons that caused the fission, and it decays to a stable element. The perturbation produces the product that opposes the perturbation. This is the same feedback architecture that holds oxygen at 20.946% in our atmosphere.

This paper resolves a gap in that earlier work. The periodic table has two elements with no stable isotopes below bismuth: technetium (43) and promethium (61). The earlier paper predicted 43 but acknowledged it could not predict 61. This paper shows that 61 was always predicted — it is the diagonal of the Klein bottle, the topological surface that the eigenvalue lives on: $43.619 \times \sqrt{2} = 61.687$, floor = 61.

But the real discovery is what these two elements **do** after they decay.

Technetium-99 decays to ruthenium-99. Ruthenium is a noble metal. Chemically inert. It just sits there. The queue is processed and the product does nothing further.

Promethium-149 decays to samarium-149. Samarium-149 is **not** inert. It is the most powerful neutron absorber among all stable fission products. It captures neutrons 1,761 times more effectively than technetium-99. And it is **stable** — it does not decay. It absorbs and holds. Permanently.

Technetium is the processor — it absorbs the perturbation, converts it, and passes it through. Samarium is the shield — it absorbs the perturbation and stops it cold.

The framework then says: to clean up Chernobyl, you need both operators.

1. **Process the queue.** The long-lived fission products (Cs-137, Sr-90, Tc-99) are in the weak force's work queue. You cannot skip the queue. You can accelerate it — transmutation programmes use neutron bombardment to convert long-lived isotopes into short-lived ones that beta-decay to stability in seconds instead of centuries. The weak force still does the work. You just deliver the atoms to the operator's window faster.

2. **Shield what cannot be processed yet.** While the queue runs, biology needs protection. Samarium-149 is the shield the constraint surface produces: the second hole (Pm-61) decays to it. In reactor physics, Sm-149 is already classified as a "neutron poison" — it absorbs so many neutrons that nuclear engineers design around it. In remediation, this property makes it a shield material. Boron-10 and gadolinium-157 serve similar roles (Gd-157 has 254,000 barns — the highest of any stable isotope).

3. **Metabolise the biological damage.** While the queue processes and the shield holds, living systems must handle the radiation that gets through. The Chernobyl frogs show this is possible. They didn't filter the mutations — they metabolised them, absorbing the structural contribution (radiation resistance, melanin upregulation) and discarding the entropy payload (lethal mutations, selected out). The black fungi of Chernobyl go further: they use melanin to convert gamma radiation directly into metabolic energy. They eat the perturbation.

This is the three-layer cleanup:

- **Layer 1 (nuclear): Process.** Transmute the queue. Accelerate beta decay by neutron capture → short-lived intermediates → stable endpoints.
- **Layer 1→0 (interface): Shield.** Use the constraint surface's own neutron absorbers (Sm-149, Gd-157, B-10) to block neutron flux from reaching biology.
- **Layer 0 (biological): Metabolise.** Support and deploy biological systems that decompose radiation damage into structural adaptation and discardable entropy.

None of this is the constraint surface doing something new. It is the constraint surface's existing architecture — processor, shield, boundary — applied to the specific problem of a containment breach. The repair station was always there. The shield was always there. The biological metabolisation was always there. They were never connected because nuclear physics, materials science, and radiation ecology are separate fields. The eigenvalue tower connects them.

## §1 — Resolving Boundary #4: The Klein Bottle Diagonal

### 1.1 The Two Holes in the Periodic Table

Below bismuth ($Z = 83$), exactly two elements have no stable isotopes:

| Element | $Z$ | Longest-lived isotope | $t_{1/2}$ | Decay modes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Technetium | 43 | Tc-98 | $4.2 \times 10^{6}$ yr | $\beta^{-}$ |
| Promethium | 61 | Pm-145 | 17.7 yr | EC |

Both are odd-$Z$. Both are flanked by even-$Z$ neighbours whose stable isobars block them via the Mattauch rule (Mattauch, 1934). Both decay exclusively by weak-force channels. No alpha decay. No spontaneous fission. Pure $\beta^{\pm}$ and EC.

The Klein bottle eigenvalue paper (Carpenter, 2026k) established:

$$\beta^{-1} + S \cdot \beta = 4\pi^{2} + \pi + 1 \qquad \Longrightarrow \qquad \beta^{-1} = 43.619\,053$$

$$\lfloor \beta^{-1} \rfloor = 43 = \text{Tc}$$

That paper's Boundary #4 stated: *"Technetium ($Z = 43$) and promethium ($Z = 61$). The tower predicts 43 but not 61. A complete theory would need to account for both."*

### 1.2 The Diagonal

The Klein bottle is topologically the quotient of a square with identification $(x, 0) \sim (x, 1)$ and $(0, y) \sim (1, 1-y)$. It can be constructed by gluing two Möbius strips along their single boundary (Stillwell, 1993). It is a non-orientable surface with Euler characteristic 0 and no boundary curve.

If $\beta^{-1}$ is the eigenvalue of one layer of the Klein bottle — the length scale of one constituent Möbius strip — then the diagonal through the two-layer structure is:

$$\beta^{-1}_{\text{diag}} = \beta^{-1} \sqrt{2} = 43.619\,053 \times 1.414\,214 = 61.686\,656$$

$$\lfloor \beta^{-1}_{\text{diag}} \rfloor = 61 = \text{Pm}$$

The $\sqrt{2}$ factor is the Pythagorean diagonal of two equal orthogonal components — the geometric consequence of traversing both Möbius layers simultaneously. The Klein bottle's non-orientable structure has two incommensurable circuits (one preserves orientation, one reverses it). The diagonal crosses both.

This resolves Boundary #4. The eigenvalue tower does predict $Z = 61$. Promethium is the Klein bottle's diagonal eigenvalue floor, the same way technetium is its principal eigenvalue floor.

### 1.3 The Sub-Tower

The golden ratio $\varphi = (1 + \sqrt{5})/2 = 1.618\,034$ appears throughout the constraint surface as the scaling constant of the logarithmic spiral (Carpenter, 2026q). Applying it to the diagonal:

$$\beta^{-1}_{\text{bound}} = \beta^{-1}\sqrt{2}\cdot\varphi = 61.687 \times 1.618 = 99.811$$

$$\lfloor \beta^{-1}_{\text{bound}} \rfloor = 99 \approx 100$$

Fermium ($Z = 100$) is the element where the neutron capture mechanism terminates. In the r-process (rapid neutron capture in supernovae and neutron star mergers), nuclei capture neutrons faster than they beta-decay. But at $Z \approx 100$, spontaneous fission becomes dominant — nuclei fission faster than they capture. The constructive mechanism of Level 1 (build heavier elements by adding neutrons) reaches its boundary. You cannot build past fermium by neutron capture alone.

The sub-tower within the Klein bottle eigenvalue:

$$\beta^{-1} \xrightarrow{\times\sqrt{2}} \beta^{-1}\sqrt{2} \xrightarrow{\times\varphi} \beta^{-1}\sqrt{2}\varphi$$

$$43 \longrightarrow 61 \longrightarrow \sim\!100$$

$$\text{Processor} \longrightarrow \text{Shield producer} \longrightarrow \text{Boundary}$$

## §2 — The Processor and the Shield

### 2.1 What Tc-99 Does

Technetium-99 is the dominant fission product at mass 99. Its thermal neutron capture cross-section is $\sigma = 22.8$ b. It absorbs a neutron and becomes Tc-100, which beta-decays to stable Ru-100 in 15.8 seconds.

$$^{99}\text{Tc} + n \to {^{100}\text{Tc}} \xrightarrow{\beta^{-}}{^{100}\text{Ru (stable)}} \quad (t_{1/2} = 15.8\text{ s})$$

The Tc chain: **absorb → process → release (inert)**. Ruthenium is a platinum-group metal. Chemically unreactive. The processed perturbation sits there, done.

### 2.2 What Pm-149 Does

Promethium-149 is a fission product with yield of $\sim 1\%$ per fission. It beta-decays to samarium-149 with $t_{1/2} = 53.1$ hours.

$$^{149}\text{Pm} \xrightarrow{\beta^{-}} {^{149}\text{Sm (stable)}} \quad (t_{1/2} = 53.1\text{ hr})$$

The Pm chain: **process → release (active shield)**.

### 2.3 What Sm-149 Does

Samarium-149 has a thermal neutron capture cross-section of:

$$\sigma_{n,\gamma}(^{149}\text{Sm}) = 40{,}140 \pm 600 \text{ b}$$

This is **1,761 times larger** than Tc-99's cross-section. Among stable fission products, Sm-149 is the most powerful neutron absorber. It is exceeded only by Gd-157 ($\sigma = 254{,}000$ b) and Xe-135 ($\sigma = 2{,}650{,}000$ b, but Xe-135 is unstable with $t_{1/2} = 9.14$ hr and is a gas).

Sm-149 is:
- **Stable** — it does not decay. Once formed, it persists.
- **Solid** — lanthanide metal, not a gas. It stays where you put it.
- **Absorptive** — captures thermal neutrons with extraordinary efficiency.
- **Self-produced by fission** — Pm-149 is a fission product; its decay produces Sm-149 automatically.

When Sm-149 absorbs a neutron, it becomes Sm-150 (stable, $\sigma = 104$ b — still absorptive but 386× less so). The shield weakens with use but does not break. Each absorption converts one Sm-149 into one Sm-150 and removes one neutron from the flux permanently.

$${^{149}\text{Sm}} + n \to {^{150}\text{Sm (stable)}} + \gamma$$

No beta decay. No transmutation. The neutron is swallowed. The flux drops. The perturbation is blocked.

### 2.4 The Complementary Architecture

| Property | **Tc-99** (processor) | **Sm-149** (shield) |
|---|---|---|
| Stable? | No (all Tc unstable) | **Yes** |
| $\sigma_{n,\gamma}$ | 22.8 b | **40,140 b** |
| After neutron capture | Decays (Tc-100 → Ru-100, 15.8 s) | **Stays stable** (Sm-150) |
| Product | Inert (Ru) | Still absorptive (Sm-150, 104 b) |
| Mechanism | Absorb → process → release | **Absorb → hold** |
| Le Chatelier role | Dampens flux by processing | **Dampens flux by blocking** |
| Eigenvalue | $\lfloor\beta^{-1}\rfloor = 43$ | Product of $\lfloor\beta^{-1}\sqrt{2}\rfloor = 61$ |
| Timescale | Geological ($t_{1/2} = 211{,}100$ yr) | **Permanent** (stable) |

The constraint surface produces two complementary operators from the same eigenvalue:
- The **processor** absorbs the perturbation, converts it via beta decay, and releases the result as inert material.
- The **shield** absorbs the perturbation and holds it. No processing. No release. Just absorption.

Processing is slow (the weak force's coupling rate). Shielding is immediate ($\sigma$ is a capture probability per unit flux — it acts at the speed of the incoming neutron).

## §3 — The Queue at Chernobyl and Fukushima

### 3.1 What Was Released

The Chernobyl accident (April 26, 1986) released the contents of a 1,000 MWe RBMK-1000 reactor core into the atmosphere. The Fukushima Daiichi accident (March 11, 2011) released material from three BWR reactor cores (Units 1, 2, 3) following tsunami-induced station blackout.

The persistent contamination at both sites is dominated by:

| Isotope | $t_{1/2}$ | Decay | Activity driver | Chernobyl release estimate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $^{137}$Cs | 30.17 yr | $\beta^{-}$ → $^{137}$Ba | Dominant external dose for decades | ~85 PBq |
| $^{90}$Sr | 28.8 yr | $\beta^{-}$ → $^{90}$Y → $^{90}$Zr | Bone-seeking; internal dose | ~10 PBq |
| $^{134}$Cs | 2.065 yr | $\beta^{-}$, EC → $^{134}$Ba, $^{134}$Xe | Significant first few years; now negligible | ~54 PBq |
| $^{131}$I | 8.02 d | $\beta^{-}$ → $^{131}$Xe | Thyroid dose in first weeks | ~1,760 PBq |
| $^{241}$Am | 432.2 yr | $\alpha$ → $^{237}$Np | Growing from $^{241}$Pu decay; future concern | Ingrowth |
| $^{239}$Pu | 24,110 yr | $\alpha$ → $^{235}$U | Long-lived hot particles | ~0.013 PBq |
| $^{99}$Tc | 211,100 yr | $\beta^{-}$ → $^{99}$Ru | Long-lived, mobile in groundwater | Not dominant at surface |

Every element-changing step in these chains is beta decay. The queue is the same queue described in the companion paper (Carpenter, 2026n). Every isotope is between its current identity and its final identity. The distance is measured in beta decays. The time is the half-life.

### 3.2 The Current Situation

**Chernobyl (40 years post-accident, 2026):**
- Cs-137: ~52% decayed (one half-life + 10 years). Activity roughly halved from peak.
- Sr-90: ~55% decayed. Similarly halved.
- Exclusion zone: 2,600 km². Human habitation prohibited. Wildlife has recolonised.
- New Safe Confinement (NSC): completed 2016, designed life 100 years. Contains ~200 tonnes of corium.
- Estimated full cleanup: no timeline. Current strategy is containment and decay.

**Fukushima (15 years post-accident, 2026):**
- Cs-137: ~28% decayed. Still dominant surface contaminant.
- Sr-90: ~29% decayed.
- Contaminated water: >1.3 million tonnes stored in tanks (ALPS-treated). Tritium release approved 2023.
- Fuel debris removal: first attempts 2024–25 with robotic arm. Estimated 880 tonnes of corium across 3 units.
- Estimated decommissioning: 30–40 years (TEPCO timeline); realistically longer.

### 3.3 The Queue in Constraint-Surface Terms

The queue at Chernobyl has been running for 40 years. In that time:
- $^{131}$I: queue complete (8-day half-life, >10 half-lives ago)
- $^{134}$Cs: queue complete (2-year half-life, ~20 half-lives ago)
- $^{137}$Cs: queue ~50% complete. One half-life processed. One remaining.
- $^{90}$Sr: queue ~50% complete. Same.
- $^{239}$Pu: queue ~0.1% complete. 24,110-year half-life; Chernobyl plutonium will be in the queue for ~250,000 years.
- $^{241}$Am: **growing** — produced by $^{241}$Pu decay ($t_{1/2} = 14.3$ yr). Am-241 activity is currently *increasing* at Chernobyl and will peak around 2060.

The constraint surface does not provide a shortcut. The weak force operates at its own coupling rate. What the framework provides is the **architecture of the solution** — not faster processing, but the identification of the three operators (process, shield, metabolise) and their deployment order.

## §4 — The Three-Layer Remediation Architecture

### 4.1 Layer 1 — Process: Transmutation

**Principle:** Accelerate the queue by converting long-lived isotopes into short-lived ones that beta-decay to stability quickly.

**Mechanism:** Neutron bombardment shifts isotopes to neighbours with shorter half-lives. The weak force still performs the transmutation. The strong force (neutron capture) and engineering (neutron sources) deliver atoms to the operator's window faster.

| Target | Reaction | Product | Product $t_{1/2}$ | Speedup |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $^{99}$Tc | $^{99}$Tc$(n,\gamma)^{100}$Tc | $^{100}$Ru (stable) | 15.8 s (via Tc-100) | $4 \times 10^{11}$× |
| $^{129}$I | $^{129}$I$(n,\gamma)^{130}$I | $^{130}$Xe (stable) | 12.4 hr (via I-130) | $1.1 \times 10^{10}$× |
| $^{137}$Cs | $^{137}$Cs$(n,\gamma)^{138}$Cs | $^{138}$Ba (stable) | 33.4 min (via Cs-138) | $4.7 \times 10^{8}$× |
| $^{90}$Sr | Difficult — low $\sigma$ and unfavourable daughter | — | — | Not practical by $n$-capture |

**Current programmes:** MYRRHA (Belgium, ADS, construction ~2027–28), JAEA (Japan, post-Fukushima fast reactor R&D), INL/ANL (USA, VTR concept). All target the same mechanism: strong-force rearrangement → weak-force completion.

**The constraint-surface reading:** Transmutation does not bypass the operator. It delivers the work to the operator's window more efficiently. The operator (beta decay) still does the transmutation. You cannot skip Level 1 processing. You can optimise delivery.

**Practical application to Chernobyl/Fukushima:** Transmutation requires separated, concentrated isotope streams. This is feasible for reprocessed spent fuel in storage casks. It is **not** feasible for isotopes already dispersed across landscape soil. The queue in the soil must run at the operator's natural rate. Transmutation applies to the contained inventory (fuel debris, contaminated water concentrates), not the environmental dispersal.

### 4.2 Layer 1→0 Interface — Shield: Neutron Absorbers

**Principle:** Block neutron flux from reaching biology. Use the constraint surface's own shield materials — the products of the second eigenvalue hole.

**The samarium family and its neighbours:**

| Isotope | $Z$ | $\sigma_{n,\gamma}$ (barns) | Stable? | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $^{149}$Sm | 62 | 40,140 | Yes | Primary shield (fission-produced) |
| $^{151}$Eu | 63 | 9,184 | Yes | Secondary shield (Sm-151 decay product) |
| $^{157}$Gd | 64 | 254,000 | Yes | Ultimate shield (highest $\sigma$ of any stable isotope) |
| $^{10}$B | 5 | 3,835 | Yes | Standard control rod / shielding material |
| $^{113}$Cd | 48 | 20,600 | Yes | Established shielding material |

Note the cluster: Sm (62), Eu (63), Gd (64) — three consecutive lanthanides, all with extraordinary neutron absorption cross-sections. The eigenvalue diagonal ($\beta^{-1}\sqrt{2} \approx 61.7$) lands at the threshold of this cluster. The shield is not one element. It is a neighbourhood — the lanthanide neutron absorber peak centred on the diagonal eigenvalue.

**Practical application:**

1. **Neutron shielding of fuel debris.** During Fukushima decommissioning, robotic retrieval of corium encounters criticality risk — the fuel debris may still contain sufficient fissile material for localised criticality if geometry changes during retrieval. Borated water is the standard countermeasure. Gadolinium-loaded water or Sm/Gd ceramic inserts would provide orders-of-magnitude more absorption per unit mass.

2. **Environmental barrier materials.** Permeable reactive barriers (PRBs) for groundwater contamination are established technology (e.g., zero-valent iron for Cr(VI)). Sm/Gd-doped PRBs for neutron-active contamination plumes would absorb neutron flux from transuranic hot particles, reducing activation of surrounding soil minerals.

3. **Personal shielding for remediation workers.** Gd₂O₃-loaded polymers and concretes for neutron shielding in decommissioning environments. Already in use at CERN and in medical physics. Application to Chernobyl/Fukushima worker protection is direct.

### 4.3 Layer 0 — Metabolise: Biological Remediation

**Principle:** Living systems cannot process Level 1 (nuclear) perturbations directly. They cannot accelerate beta decay or transmute isotopes. But they CAN metabolise the Level 0 (chemical/biological) consequences of Level 1 perturbations.

**The frog evidence:**

Car et al. (2023, *BMC Biology*) documented tree frogs in the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone that have lived under chronic radiation for 40 years. Key findings:
- Darker melanin pigmentation (radiation shielding)
- Stop-gained mutations (genetic entropy accumulation)
- Impaired metabolism but **viable populations**
- Significantly different from clean-zone conspecifics

The frog doctrine (Carpenter, 2026, internal) formalises this as **metabolisation, not filtration**:

$$M = M_{\text{structural}} + M_{\text{entropy}}$$

The organism decomposes each mutation into:
- $M_{\text{structural}}$: novel valid paths — radiation resistance, enhanced DNA repair, melanin upregulation, altered cell cycle checkpoints
- $M_{\text{entropy}}$: lethal mutations, non-viable developmental programmes — selected out

The constraint surface at Level 0 cannot oppose the Level 1 perturbation directly. But it can metabolise the Level 0 consequences. The frogs cannot stop Cs-137 from emitting beta particles. They can rewire their biochemistry to survive the beta particles while Cs-137 decays.

**The fungal evidence:**

Dadachova et al. (2007, *PLoS One*) demonstrated that melanised fungi (*Cryptococcus neoformans*, *Wangiella dermatitidis*, *Cladosporium sphaerospermum*) exposed to ionising radiation:
- Grew **faster** under radiation than without it
- Showed increased electron transfer in melanin under radiation
- Converted gamma radiation into metabolic energy via melanin-mediated electron transport

Zhdanova et al. (2000) documented radiotrophic fungi **growing toward** radiation sources in the Chernobyl reactor building. The fungi do not merely survive Level 1 perturbation. They eat it.

This is the extreme expression of the metabolisation operator: $M_{\text{structural}}$ is so thoroughly absorbed that radiation becomes an energy source. $M_{\text{entropy}}$ is discarded (dead hyphae, non-viable spores). The net result is a biological system that converts the perturbation into growth.

**Practical application — Bioremediation:**

1. **Mycoremediation with radiotrophic fungi.** Deploy melanised fungal species on contaminated surfaces. The fungi:
   - Accumulate radionuclides from soil via hyphal networks (biosorption)
   - Convert a fraction of the radiation to metabolic energy
   - Concentrate dispersed contamination into harvestable biomass
   - Reduce bioavailability of radionuclides to plants and animals

   This does not accelerate decay (the queue still runs at the weak force's rate). It **concentrates and immobilises** the queue — moving isotopes from dispersed, bioavailable forms in soil into concentrated, harvestable forms in fungal biomass. The dispersed queue becomes a contained queue.

2. **Phytoremediation with hyperaccumulator plants.** Sunflowers (*Helianthus annuus*) and Indian mustard (*Brassica juncea*) accumulate Cs-137 and Sr-90 from soil. Deployed at Chernobyl and Fukushima since the 1990s. The mechanism: plants extract radionuclides via root uptake, concentrate them in harvestable above-ground biomass, reducing soil contamination. The biomass is then collected and treated as concentrated waste (smaller volume, easier to contain).

3. **Bacterial bioremediation.** *Deinococcus radiodurans* — the most radiation-resistant organism known — survives doses >5,000 Gy (lethal human dose: ~5 Gy). Its extreme DNA repair capacity (multiple copies of genome, rapid reassembly after radiation-induced shattering) demonstrates that Level 0 repair mechanisms, when sufficiently upregulated, can maintain biological function under Level 1 perturbation rates that destroy other organisms. *D. radiodurans* has been engineered to express mercury reductase and toluene dioxygenase for combined radioactive/chemical waste remediation (Brim et al., 2000).

4. **Melanin as radiation-protective material.** Synthetic melanin or melanin-producing organisms applied as coatings on contaminated surfaces. Melanin absorbs and dissipates ionising radiation energy. Fungal melanin coatings on interior surfaces of Chernobyl's NSC could reduce radiation-driven material degradation of the containment structure itself.

### 4.4 The Three-Layer Deployment

For a site like Chernobyl or Fukushima, the three layers deploy in parallel:

| Layer | Target | Mechanism | Timescale | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Process** | Contained fuel debris, concentrated waste streams | Transmutation (ADS, fast reactor, neutron sources) | Decades (engineering); seconds (nuclear) | Requires isotope separation and dedicated facilities |
| **Shield** | Fuel retrieval operations, groundwater barriers, worker protection | Sm/Gd/B neutron absorbers in structural materials | Immediate (materials exist) | Proven technology; deployment is logistics |
| **Metabolise** | Dispersed environmental contamination | Fungi, plants, bacteria — concentrate and immobilise | Years to decades per cycle | Demonstrated at both sites; scaling is the challenge |

The layers are not sequential. They operate simultaneously at different scales:
- **Processing** handles the concentrated inventory (the bulk of the radioactive mass).
- **Shielding** protects biology during processing and at environmental interfaces.
- **Metabolisation** handles what escapes containment — the dispersed fraction that cannot be collected for transmutation.

## §5 — The Le Chatelier Architecture at Both Scales

### 5.1 Nuclear Scale

The companion paper (Carpenter, 2026n) established the Le Chatelier feedback at the nuclear scale:

$$\text{Fission} \to \text{excess neutrons} \to \text{Tc-99 produced} \to \text{neutron absorption} \to \text{flux dampened}$$

This paper adds the second arm:

$$\text{Fission} \to \text{Pm-149 produced} \to \text{Pm-149 decays (53.1 hr)} \to \text{Sm-149 formed} \to \text{neutron absorption (40,140 b)} \to \text{flux dampened}$$

The Tc arm is the **slow processor** — absorbs a neutron, decays the product to stable Ru, removes one neutron permanently. Geological timescale for natural decay; seconds for transmutation-assisted.

The Sm arm is the **fast shield** — Pm decays in hours (not millennia), producing stable Sm-149 which absorbs neutrons immediately and permanently without needing to decay further. The shield is available within days of fission. The processor takes millennia.

In reactor physics, the Sm-149 poison effect is well-known: after reactor shutdown, Pm-149 continues decaying to Sm-149 for days, increasing the neutron absorption. Reactor restart requires overcoming this "samarium poisoning." This is Le Chatelier operating on the shutdown transient: the perturbation (fission) has stopped, but the shield (Sm-149) continues building, ensuring the perturbation cannot easily restart.

### 5.2 Biological Scale

The frog doctrine identifies the same architecture at the biological scale:

$$\text{Radiation} \to \text{mutation} \to \text{selection for resistant variants} \to \text{population adapts} \to \text{damage reduced}$$

The perturbation (radiation) produces the product (radiation-resistant organisms) that opposes the perturbation (adapted biology survives the radiation that killed the non-adapted). The Chernobyl frogs **are** the Le Chatelier counter-product at the biological scale.

The Black fungi take this further:

$$\text{Radiation} \to \text{melanin activation} \to \text{energy harvest} \to \text{growth toward radiation source} \to \text{radiation absorbed by fungal biomass rather than other biology}$$

The perturbation does not merely produce resistance. It produces a biological system that **consumes** the perturbation. The fungus converts the perturbation into its own growth, which increases its capacity to absorb more perturbation. Positive feedback within the Le Chatelier negative feedback loop — the counter-product amplifies itself.

## §6 — The Practical Cleanup Guide

### 6.1 Immediate Actions (Months)

**For a Chernobyl/Fukushima-scale site:**

1. **Characterise the queue.** Gamma spectrometry survey of the site to map isotope-specific activity. Identify Cs-137 and Sr-90 hotspots. Distinguish between dispersed contamination (soil, biota) and concentrated contamination (fuel debris, sludge).

2. **Deploy biological metabolisers.** Inoculate contaminated soil with melanised fungal consortia (*Cladosporium*, *Wangiella*, *Cryptococcus*). Plant hyperaccumulators (sunflower, *Brassica*) in contaminated zones. Purpose: begin concentrating dispersed radionuclides into harvestable biomass. This is passive, low-cost, and can cover large areas.

3. **Shield the workers.** Gd₂O₃-loaded concrete and polymer composites for shielding enclosures. B₄C-impregnated flexible mats for temporary work positions. Standard practice in nuclear decommissioning; ensure deployment at all occupied positions near fuel debris.

### 6.2 Short-Term Actions (Years)

4. **Harvest and concentrate.** Collect fungal/plant biomass after growing season. Incinerate in controlled facility with HEPA + activated carbon filtration. Ash contains concentrated radionuclides at much higher specific activity than original soil — smaller volume, easier to vitrify or encapsulate. Repeat annually.

5. **Soil removal and washing.** For highest-activity hotspots where bioremediation is too slow: mechanical topsoil removal (top 5–15 cm captures >90% of Cs-137 and Sr-90 in undisturbed profiles). Soil washing to separate contaminated fines from bulk material. Contaminated fines: volume-reduced waste for long-term storage or transmutation feed.

6. **Water treatment.** Cs-137 and Sr-90 removal from contaminated groundwater using zeolite ion exchange (clinoptilolite for Cs, titanosilicate for Sr). Proven at both Chernobyl and Fukushima. Loaded zeolite becomes concentrated waste — smaller volume, contained queue.

### 6.3 Medium-Term Actions (Decades)

7. **Transmutation feed preparation.** Separated Cs-137, Sr-90, Tc-99, and minor actinides from concentrated waste streams undergo reprocessing into transmutation-compatible target forms (oxide pellets, cermet pins, or liquid fuel for molten salt reactor concepts).

8. **Transmutation.** Irradiate targets in purpose-built facilities:
   - ADS (MYRRHA-type) for minor actinides and long-lived fission products
   - Fast reactors for transuranic burning
   - Each target isotope captures a neutron and beta-decays through a short-lived intermediate to a stable endpoint

9. **Fuel debris retrieval.** At Fukushima: continue robotic retrieval of corium from Units 1–3 under neutron-shielded conditions (Gd-loaded water, B₄C inserts). Extracted debris → reprocessing → transmutation or vitrification for geological disposal.

### 6.4 Long-Term Actions (Centuries)

10. **Geological disposal of residuals.** Whatever cannot be transmuted (isotopes with unfavourable cross-sections, traces below separation efficiency) goes to deep geological repository. The remaining queue runs at the natural rate. The operator finishes its work underground, isolated from the biosphere by geological barriers.

11. **Monitored natural recovery.** The exclusion zone itself continues its biological metabolisation. The Chernobyl Exclusion Zone is already the largest accidental rewilding experiment in Europe. The constraint surface's Level 0 operators — fungal networks, adapted wildlife, forest succession — continue concentrating and immobilising radionuclides in biomass and organic matter, reducing their bioavailability to new organisms entering the zone.

12. **Queue completion.** After ~300 years: Cs-137 and Sr-90 drop below background (10 half-lives). After ~2 million years: Tc-99 completes. After ~250,000 years: Pu-239 effectively complete. The queue is finite. The operator is total. The resolution is guaranteed.

## §8 — Boundaries

1. **The $\sqrt{2}$ factor is a geometric identification, not a derivation.** The claim that $\beta^{-1}\sqrt{2}$ corresponds to the Klein bottle's diagonal rests on the topological construction (two Möbius strips glued at boundary). Field deployment of radiotrophic fungi for large-scale remediation has not been conducted. 7. **The Chernobyl frog evidence is observational, not experimental.** Car et al. (2023) document phenotypic and genetic differences between exclusion-zone and clean-zone populations. The metabolisation interpretation (absorb $M_{\text{structural}}$, discard $M_{\text{entropy}}$) is this framework's reading of the data, not the authors' framing.
