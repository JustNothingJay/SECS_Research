No other physical constant has been tested as extensively. $c$ is invariant. $\mathcal{G}_0$ requires invariance. The correspondence holds.

The constitution is structural, not incidental.

**Self-enforcing.** Tolman [11] demonstrated that faster-than-light signalling permits the construction of a "Tolman antitelephone" — a device that sends messages backward in time. In special relativity, if signal velocity $v > c$, there exist reference frames in which the signal arrives before it is sent. This produces causal paradoxes that are not merely counterintuitive but logically self-contradictory: the sender can receive a reply to a message they then decide not to send.

In the algebra: $\mathcal{G}_0$ violation breaks the Irreversibility of Collapse [1, §2]. The determined set — the history of all collapsed inputs — must be monotonically increasing. ## The Lorentz Factor as Admissibility Function $\alpha$

### The Admissibility Function

The algebra [1, §3] defines $\alpha(s)$ as the admissibility function: a mapping from potential inputs $s \in \mathcal{P}$ to $\{\text{admissible}, \text{inadmissible}\}$. Inadmissible inputs are vetoed. The admissibility function partitions the possibility space into the reachable (within the constitution) and the unreachable (beyond it).

In the physical domain, admissibility is not binary at a distance — it degrades continuously as the system approaches the constitutional boundary. The cost of maintaining a state close to the boundary exceeds the cost of a state far from it. This motivates extending the admissibility function to a continuous **admissibility cost function**: $\alpha(s) \to [1, \infty)$, with $\alpha = 1$ at rest and $\alpha \to \infty$ at the boundary. This extension preserves the algebra's binary boundary ($\alpha = \infty$ at the veto) while resolving the interior into a measurable cost gradient.

### The Lorentz Factor as Admissibility Cost

The Lorentz factor:

$$\gamma(v) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}}$$

$\gamma$ maps the velocity $v$ of a massive object to a dimensionless scaling factor with the following properties:

1. $\gamma(0) = 1$: at rest, no admissibility cost
2. $\gamma$ is monotonically increasing in $|v|$: moving faster costs more
3. $\gamma \to \infty$ as $v \to c$: the cost diverges at the constitutional boundary
4. $\gamma$ is undefined at $v = c$ for massive systems: the boundary is not reachable

These are precisely the properties of the admissibility cost function as applied to the algebra's constitutional constraint. The Lorentz factor IS $\alpha$ for the velocity domain.

### Physical Consequences as Admissibility Costs

The Lorentz factor manifests in three measurable ways, each interpretable as an admissibility cost:

**Relativistic momentum.** $p = \gamma mv$. At low velocities, momentum scales linearly with speed. As $v \to c$, the $\gamma$ factor causes momentum to diverge — each increment of velocity requires disproportionately more force. This is the admissibility cost as inertial resistance — the system resists being pushed further toward the boundary. Confirmed by particle accelerator measurements [17].

**Time dilation.** $\Delta t' = \gamma \Delta t$. Moving clocks run slower. As $v \to c$, time for the moving system approaches a standstill. This is the admissibility cost as temporal compression — the system's internal processing rate decreases as it approaches the boundary. Confirmed by muon lifetime measurements [18], GPS satellite corrections [19], and direct atomic clock comparisons [20].

**Length contraction.** $L' = L / \gamma$. Moving objects contract in the direction of motion. As $v \to c$, spatial extent approaches zero. This is the admissibility cost as dimensional compression — the system loses spatial degrees of freedom as it approaches the boundary. Derivable from the Lorentz transformation; confirmed indirectly through consistency of all relativistic predictions.

### The Veto

At $v = c$ for massive systems: $\gamma$ is undefined. Infinite mass, zero time, zero length. This is the veto $V$ — the algebraic equivalent of $\alpha(s) = \text{inadmissible}$ for all states. No physical process can place a massive object at $v = c$.

The veto is not enforced by a barrier. It is enforced by asymptotic cost: the energy required diverges. To accelerate any massive object to $c$ would require infinite energy. The veto is a consequence of the cost structure, not a wall at the boundary.

## Iron-56: The Nuclear Fixed Point

### The Binding Energy Curve

The nuclear binding energy per nucleon $B/A$ — the average energy required to remove a single nucleon from a nucleus — varies by element. Empirically measured and theoretically described by the semi-empirical mass formula [21] [22]:

$$B = a_V A - a_S A^{2/3} - a_C \frac{Z(Z-1)}{A^{1/3}} - a_A \frac{(A - 2Z)^2}{A} + \delta(A, Z)$$

The binding energy per nucleon $B/A$ peaks at Iron-56 ($^{56}$Fe) and its neighbours (Ni-62 has the highest $B/A$ by a small margin, but Fe-56 is the relevant end-point for stellar nucleosynthesis due to nuclear reaction pathway accessibility).

### Iron as Nuclear $\omega_0$

Iron-56 is the unique point toward which all nuclear energy-releasing processes contract:

- **Fusion** (combining lighter nuclei): exothermic for all elements lighter than iron. Stars fuse hydrogen to helium to carbon to oxygen to silicon to iron. At iron, fusion becomes endothermic. The contraction mapping has reached its fixed point.
- **Fission** (splitting heavier nuclei): exothermic for all elements heavier than iron (specifically, above mass number ~56–62). Nuclear reactors split uranium and plutonium toward the iron group. The contraction mapping runs in the opposite direction but converges to the same point.

Iron is $\omega_0$ of nuclear binding: the unique attractor in both directions. No nuclear process starting from iron can release energy. The fixed point is stable.

### Stellar Nucleosynthesis as Contraction Mapping

Stars execute the fusion contraction mapping over their lifetimes. A massive star ($\geq 8 M_\odot$) fuses progressively heavier elements in an onion-shell structure [23] [24]:

$$\text{H} \xrightarrow{\sim 10^7 \text{ yr}} \text{He} \xrightarrow{\sim 10^6 \text{ yr}} \text{C} \xrightarrow{\sim 10^3 \text{ yr}} \text{Ne} \xrightarrow{\sim 1 \text{ yr}} \text{O} \xrightarrow{\sim 0.5 \text{ yr}} \text{Si} \xrightarrow{\sim 1 \text{ day}} \text{Fe}$$

The contraction accelerates: each stage runs faster than the last. The ratio of durations is approximately $10{,}000{,}000 : 1{,}000{,}000 : 1{,}000 : 1 : 0.5 : 0.003$, spanning ten orders of magnitude from hydrogen burning to silicon burning. This acceleration is consistent with a contraction mapping where the contraction coefficient $q$ decreases as the system approaches the fixed point — each iteration covers a larger fraction of the remaining distance.

### Supernova as Generative Veto

At the iron core: fusion ceases. No energy source remains. The core exceeds the Chandrasekhar mass ($\sim 1.4 M_\odot$). Electron degeneracy pressure cannot support the core. Gravitational collapse occurs in milliseconds. The core rebounds (or forms a neutron star/black hole), and the resulting shockwave — enhanced by neutrino energy deposition — ejects the stellar envelope.

This is the veto $V$ in the algebra. The system reached $\omega_0$ and cannot proceed further by the current process. The cycle terminates. But the veto is generative [3]: the supernova ejects every element the star produced (carbon, oxygen, nitrogen, silicon, iron, and via rapid neutron capture during the explosion itself, elements heavier than iron — gold, platinum, uranium) into the interstellar medium. New stars and planetary systems form from this enriched material. The next generation begins with a richer palette.

The generative fixed point [3]: collapse is not destruction. It is the precondition for the next cycle. The star collapses to iron. The iron becomes the foundation: it enriches the next generation's chemistry, becomes the core of rocky planets, and — critically — becomes the cofactor for biological oxygen sensing in the organisms that evolve on those planets.

1. PHD hydroxylates specific proline residues on HIF-1α
2. This requires three substrates: molecular $O_2$, 2-oxoglutarate, and **Fe$^{2+}$** (ferrous iron)
3. Hydroxylated HIF-1α is recognised by VHL → ubiquitination → proteasomal degradation
4. Under hypoxia: insufficient $O_2$ → PHD inactive → HIF-1α accumulates → hypoxic gene programme activates
5. Under iron deficiency: insufficient Fe$^{2+}$ → PHD inactive → HIF-1α accumulates → **pseudohypoxia** (hypoxic response despite adequate $O_2$)

The iron requirement is not incidental. It is catalytic: the Fe$^{2+}$ ion sits in the active site of PHD and directly participates in the hydroxylation reaction by activating molecular $O_2$ [27]. Without iron, the enzyme cannot function. Period.

## Boundaries and What This Paper Does Not Claim

1. **The mapping identifies structural isomorphism, not identity.** The claim is that $c$ satisfies the formal properties of $\mathcal{G}_0$ and $\gamma$ satisfies the formal properties of $\alpha$. The claim is NOT that special relativity is "really about" collapse algebra, or that the algebra "explains" relativity. Relativity has its own complete theoretical framework. The mapping identifies a shared mathematical structure.

2. **The photon interpretation is speculative.** Describing the photon as "$\mathcal{G}_0$ in motion" is a structural observation, not a physical prediction. QED describes the photon completely without this interpretation. The interpretation is offered because it is structurally consistent and may suggest connections to gauge theory; it does not add to or modify QED.

3. **The iron convergence is a pattern observation, not a derivation.** That Fe-56 is both the nuclear binding energy maximum and the PHD cofactor is a physical fact with established explanations (nuclear physics for the former, biochemistry for the latter). That this constitutes a "convergence of fixed points" is an interpretation within the algebraic framework. The interpretation identifies a connection that is genuinely present but not commonly noted. Furthermore, iron is not the only biologically essential transition metal: zinc, copper, manganese, cobalt, and molybdenum all serve as enzyme cofactors in essential metabolic pathways. Iron is highlighted because it is the most abundant, constitutes the PHD cofactor directly relevant to the oxygen-sensing fixed point, and occupies the unique position of nuclear binding energy maximum — but a complete treatment would need to account for the broader transition-metal landscape.

4. **$\alpha \approx 1/137$ is addressed in a companion paper.** At the time of writing, the explanatory chain terminated at the fine structure constant. A subsequent paper [32] derives $\alpha^{-1}$ from the self-consistency condition of the four-operator algebra composed in four-dimensional spacetime, obtaining $\alpha^{-1} = 137.035\,999\,177$ — within 0.08 ppb (0.3$\sigma$) of the best current measurement (Fan et al., 2023, electron $g{-}2$). The present paper identifies $\alpha$ as the deepest accessible layer of $\mathcal{G}_0$ — the constitution of the constitution. Paper [32] closes the chain by showing that this layer is not a free parameter but an eigenvalue of the algebraic structure itself.

5. **General relativity is not treated in this paper.** The mapping is limited to special relativity and nuclear physics. A general-relativistic treatment would require mapping the Einstein field equations to the algebra, particularly the relationship between $\mathcal{G}_0$ and spacetime curvature. This is reserved for future work.

6. **Quantum mechanics is not treated beyond QED context.** The measurement problem, wave function collapse, and the relationship between quantum observation and the algebra's collapse operator are not addressed here. These constitute a separate and substantial investigation.
