## Overview

This corpus contains **36 research documents** investigating a novel hypothesis: the timing of oxygen-demand spikes during fetal organ development, combined with disruptions to the HIF-1α/PHD-VHL-FIH counter-regulation axis, creates predictable vulnerability windows for latent organ injury — particularly white matter injury and OPC (oligodendrocyte precursor cell) maturation arrest.

The work was motivated by a personal case (preeclampsia, neonatal ICU, oxygen desaturations) and grew into a systematic, citation-backed investigation spanning neonatal physiology, developmental neuroscience, epigenetics, HIF molecular biology, and global health epidemiology.

A synthesis paper — *"Gestational Oxygen-Timing Alignment: A Theoretical Framework Linking Placental Hypoxia Timing to OPC Maturation Arrest and Region-Specific Myelination Deficits in Autism Spectrum Disorder"* — was submitted to **medRxiv** in March 2026 and rejected as out of scope ("not a clinical research article with new data"). The rejection was not a judgment on the merits of the work. Alternative preprint venues are being evaluated.

## Core Thesis

1. **Oxygen demand is not constant** — each fetal organ system has a peak oxygen-demand window during development
2. **These windows are staggered** — the fetus cannot build all systems simultaneously
3. **Preeclampsia disrupts supply during the critical super-window** (weeks 23–36) when the most systems overlap
4. **HIF-1α counter-regulation depends on iron, ascorbate, and 2-oxoglutarate** — all commonly deficient in pregnancy
5. **Pseudohypoxia** (HIF activation without true hypoxia) may affect 40–60% of pregnancies globally via iron deficiency
6. **The injury is latent** — normal scans at birth, symptoms emerge years later as demands exceed compromised capacity

## Testable Predictions

1. **PRISM Study** — Prospective cohort linking continuous fetal/neonatal O₂ monitoring with DTI at 2 years and neurodevelopmental outcomes at 5 years
2. **Iron-PHD Interaction** — Maternal iron status modulates HIF counter-regulation effectiveness; iron-deficient pregnancies should show elevated HIF target gene expression in cord blood
3. **Caffeine Timing Extension** — CAP trial data reanalysis by gestational age at treatment initiation should show dose-response for white matter protection
4. **LMIC Natural Experiment** — NEST360 cohorts (2019–2020 births) are now reaching developmental assessment age; O₂ availability at birth should predict neurodevelopmental trajectory
