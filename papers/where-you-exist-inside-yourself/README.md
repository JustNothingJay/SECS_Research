# Where You Exist, Inside Yourself: The Obex as Fixed Point of Biological Observation

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.19206307.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19206307)

**Author:** Jay Andrew Carpenter
**Published:** [Zenodo](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19206307) (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19206307)

## Summary

The obex -- the dorsal vagal complex at the caudal floor of the fourth ventricle -- is the unique anatomical locus where all visceral self-telemetry converges, the blood-brain barrier is absent, neural stem cells persist into adulthood, zinc arrives without filtration, and destruction is instantly lethal. The paper inverts the standard cortex-down model of observation: the readiness potential is not evidence that the brain decides before the observer, but that the decision pipeline is longer than the cortex. The obex reads the body first. The brain is the instrument, not the observer.
