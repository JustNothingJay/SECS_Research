# Neonatal Dataset Audit: Continuous SpO₂ × DTI × Language Outcomes

**Date:** 2026-03-03  
**Purpose:** Identify existing neonatal research datasets/cohorts with (1) high-resolution continuous SpO₂ recording and (2) follow-up DTI/MRI neuroimaging and/or language-specific neurodevelopmental assessment at age 2–5+ years, to determine where the proposed study (IH burden → arcuate fasciculus integrity → language outcomes) could be run retrospectively.

---

## CRITICAL FINDING: HAS THE THREE-WAY LINK BEEN DONE?

**No published study has linked all three: continuous SpO₂ → DTI white matter (arcuate fasciculus specifically) → language outcomes.**

A PubMed search for "preterm intermittent hypoxemia white matter DTI language" returned zero results. No published study as of March 2026 has combined these three data types in a single analysis. This confirms the proposed study occupies a genuine gap in the literature.

The **closest existing work** is:
- **Poets et al. (2015, JAMA)** — COT trial data: continuous SpO₂ → composite death/disability including "cognitive or language delay" at 18 months. No DTI. No tract-specific analysis.
- **Di Fiore et al. (2025, Neonatology)** — continuous SpO₂ → ASQ-3 communication domain at 12 and 24 months. No DTI. No neuroimaging of any kind.
- **VIBeS group (Melbourne)** — DTI + language outcomes in preterm children. No continuous SpO₂.

**The gap is real. No dataset currently in the published literature has all three legs of the triad.**

### 1. NeOProM Collaboration (Meta-analysis of 5 Trials)

**Source:** Askie LM et al. (2018) JAMA 319(21):2190–2201. PMID: 29872859.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cohort size** | 4,965 infants across 5 RCTs |
| **Gestational age** | <28 weeks (median 26 weeks) |
| **Component trials** | SUPPORT (US), BOOST II (UK, Australia, NZ), COT (Canada/international) |
| **Continuous SpO₂** | **Complicated — varies by trial (see individual trials below)** |
| **DTI/MRI follow-up** | **No** — not part of the NeOProM protocol |
| **Language outcomes** | **Yes** — Bayley-III cognitive or language score <85 was a component of the primary composite outcome (death or major disability at 18–24 months corrected age) |
| **Data accessibility** | Individual participant data held by respective trial groups; NeOProM coordinated via University of Sydney (Lisa Askie). Not openly accessible. Restricted. |
| **Can Study 1 run here?** | **No in its full form** — NeOProM collected SpO₂ target assignments (85–89% vs 91–95%), not necessarily raw continuous SpO₂ waveforms for IH quantification. No DTI. However, the COT sub-study (see below) is the exception. |

**Key limitation:** NeOProM was a meta-analysis of target ranges, not a monitoring study. The continuous SpO₂ data availability depends on which of the 5 individual trials stored raw waveforms.

### 1c. Canadian Oxygen Trial (COT) — ★ HIGHEST PRIORITY DATASET

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cohort size** | 1,201 enrolled; ~1,019 survived to 36 weeks PMA |
| **Source** | Schmidt B et al.; Poets et al. (2015) JAMA 314(6):595–603 (PMID: 26262797); Jensen et al. (2021) AJRCCM 204(10):1192–1199 (PMID: 34428130) |
| **Continuous SpO₂ stored?** | **YES — CONFIRMED.** Oxygen saturations sampled every 10 seconds, continuously, from within 24 hours of birth until at least 36 weeks PMA. This is the gold standard for continuous neonatal SpO₂ recording. Episodes of SpO₂ <80% for ≥1 minute were quantified. Downloaded saturation data available for a median of 68.3 days per infant. |
| **DTI/MRI follow-up** | **No direct evidence found.** Published COT follow-up focused on clinical composite outcomes, not neuroimaging. No DTI sub-study published. |
| **Language outcomes** | **Yes** — "cognitive or language delay" was a secondary outcome at 18 months corrected age. Motor impairment, cognitive delay, and language delay were analyzed separately. |
| **Data accessibility** | **Restricted.** Coordinated by McMaster University (Barbara Schmidt). Post hoc analyses have been published (Jensen et al. 2021 for BPD; Poets et al. 2015 for death/disability), indicating the data are accessible for qualified collaborations. Clinical trial registered: ISRCTN62491227 and NCT00637169. |
| **Key contact** | Barbara Schmidt (McMaster/U Penn), Robin Whyte (Dalhousie), Christian Poets (Tübingen) |
| **Can Study 1 run here?** | **PARTIALLY** — COT has the best continuous SpO₂ data available (every 10 seconds for ~2 months) and language outcomes at 18 months. Missing element: **no DTI/MRI neuroimaging**. Study 1 could run the SpO₂ → language leg but cannot test the DTI mediating pathway. |

**Assessment:** COT is the single most important existing dataset for IH burden quantification. A collaboration to add DTI follow-up in surviving COT participants (now ~15–19 years old) would be transformative but logistically very difficult.

### 2. Di Fiore / Case Western / Rainbow Babies Cohort — ★ HIGH PRIORITY

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cohort size** | ~175 infants in the 2025 publication (Di Fiore et al. 2025) |
| **Institution** | Rainbow Babies & Children's Hospital, University Hospitals Cleveland Medical Center / Case Western Reserve University |
| **Source** | Di Fiore JM et al. (2025) Neonatology 122(4):388–397. PMID: 40418912 |
| **Continuous SpO₂ stored?** | **YES — CONFIRMED.** IH events (<80% and <90%) documented from day of life 8 to 28. Continuous cardiorespiratory monitoring used. Multiple publications demonstrate they have high-resolution stored SpO₂ data. Earlier publications (Di Fiore et al. 2010, 2012) also used continuous oximetry. |
| **DTI/MRI follow-up** | **No direct evidence found.** No published Di Fiore paper includes DTI or structural MRI as outcomes. |
| **Language outcomes** | **Yes — limited.** ASQ-3 (Ages and Stages Questionnaire, 3rd edition) at 12 and 24 months corrected age, including communication domain. ASQ-3 is a **screening tool**, not a standardized language assessment. The 2025 paper found a significant association between greater % time <90% and ASQ-3 communication scores in referral range at 12 months (p=0.0158). |
| **Assessment used** | ASQ-3 (screening, >2 SD below mean for gross motor, communication, fine motor, problem solving, personal-social) |
| **Gestational age** | <31 weeks gestation |
| **Data accessibility** | **Restricted — single-center.** Would require direct collaboration with JM Di Fiore and AM Hibbs at Case Western. |
| **Key contacts** | Juliann M. Di Fiore, Anna Maria Hibbs, Richard J. Martin (Rainbow Babies/Case Western) |
| **Funding** | NHLBI K24 HL143291, U01 HL133643, U01 HL133708 |
| **Can Study 1 run here?** | **PARTIALLY** — excellent continuous SpO₂ data, some language data (ASQ-3 communication), but **no DTI/MRI**. The ASQ-3 is a screening instrument, not a diagnostic standardized test like CELF or PLS, making the language data less granular. The cohort is also relatively small (n=175). |

**Important note:** Di Fiore/Hibbs are also investigators on the **Pre-Vent study** (see below), suggesting potential for negotiating access to both cohorts through the same team.

### 3. Pre-Vent Study (Prematurity-Related Ventilatory Control) — ★ HIGH PRIORITY

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cohort size** | 717 infants |
| **Gestational age** | <29 weeks |
| **Study type** | Observational multicenter prospective cohort |
| **Source** | Ambalavanan N et al. (2023) AJRCCM 208(1):79–97. PMID: 37219236 |
| **Continuous SpO₂ stored?** | **YES — CONFIRMED.** Continuous cardiorespiratory monitoring was the core data type. IH with SpO₂ <90% and <80% were the primary physiologic predictors. High-resolution data including SpO₂ and heart rate time series were collected. |
| **Sites** | Multiple NHLBI-funded centers: University of Alabama at Birmingham, Ann & Robert H. Lurie Children's Hospital/Northwestern, Case Western/Rainbow Babies, University of Miami, Washington University St. Louis, University of Virginia, CHOP |
| **DTI/MRI follow-up** | **No direct evidence found** in published papers. The study focused on respiratory outcomes at 40 weeks PMA. Whether any sites collected MRI is unknown from published data. |
| **Language outcomes** | **No direct evidence found.** Primary outcome was respiratory status at 40 weeks PMA and death/BPD at 36 weeks PMA. Neurodevelopmental follow-up beyond respiratory outcomes is not reported in published papers. |
| **Data accessibility** | **Restricted.** NHLBI-funded (U01 grants). Data likely held at individual sites and coordinated by the Pre-Vent investigator group. Would require multi-PI collaboration agreement. |
| **Key investigators** | Namasivayam Ambalavanan (Alabama), Debra Weese-Mayer (Northwestern/Lurie), Anna Maria Hibbs (Case Western), Nelson Claure (Miami), J. Randall Moorman (Virginia), Aaron Hamvas (Northwestern), Richard Martin (Case Western), J. Randall Lake (Virginia), Phyllis Dennery (CHOP) |
| **Can Study 1 run here?** | **PARTIALLY** — excellent continuous SpO₂ data on the largest multicenter cohort (717 infants), but **no DTI and no published language outcomes**. However, because the infants are well-characterized and the study is recent (enrollment ~2017–2020), follow-up neuroimaging and language assessment could potentially be added. |

**Assessment:** Pre-Vent is the richest source of continuous high-resolution cardiorespiratory data in extremely preterm infants, with the most sophisticated analysis pipeline (HCTSA algorithms, machine learning approaches). If a DTI/language follow-up could be added to Pre-Vent participants (now age ~5–8), this would be the ideal dataset.

## NICU MONITORING SYSTEMS AND DATA STORAGE

### Which systems store continuous SpO₂ data?

**Modern NICU bedside monitors** from all major manufacturers (Masimo, Philips, GE, Dräger) are capable of outputting continuous SpO₂ at 1-second resolution. However:

1. **Data storage is not automatic in most NICUs.** Whether continuous SpO₂ is archived depends entirely on whether the NICU has a **clinical data warehouse** or **research data capture system** connected to the bedside monitors.

2. **Common data capture systems:**
   - **Philips IntelliVue/PIIC iX** — can export 1-second vital signs to research databases
   - **Masimo Root/Radical** — used in the NeOProM trials with modified firmware for blinding; Masimo's Patient SafetyNet can store data
   - **GE CARESCAPE** — can feed to iCentra or other data warehouses
   - **BedMasterEx (Excel Medical)** — third-party system that archives high-resolution bedside monitor data; used at several US academic NICUs
   - **Sickbay (Medical Informatics Corp)** — real-time data streaming platform for ICU monitoring data

3. **Pre-Vent and Di Fiore/Rainbow Babies** both use Masimo oximeters with dedicated research recording protocols, confirming that continuous high-resolution SpO₂ data CAN be systematically captured — but it requires deliberate setup.

4. **Standard format:** No universal standard exists. Data are typically stored as timestamped CSV/flat files, proprietary formats (Masimo SET, Philips XML), or in clinical data warehouses (Epic Clarity/Caboodle, Cerner warehouse). HL7 FHIR is emerging but not yet standard for high-resolution waveform data.

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Tier 1: Best Existing Opportunities

1. **COT + DTI add-on study:** The COT trial has the world's best published continuous SpO₂ dataset for preterm infants (1,019 infants, every 10 seconds, ~68 days median recording). If surviving COT participants (now age ~15–19) could be recalled for DTI and language assessment, this would be the definitive dataset. **Contact: Barbara Schmidt (McMaster/Penn), Robin Whyte, Christian Poets.**

2. **Di Fiore/Rainbow Babies + DTI add-on:** The Di Fiore group has established continuous SpO₂ recording pipelines and ASQ-3 follow-up. Adding DTI to their existing or upcoming cohorts would complete the triad. Relatively small n (175) but highly detailed SpO₂ data. They are also Pre-Vent investigators, creating a bridge. **Contact: Juliann Di Fiore, Anna Maria Hibbs, Richard Martin (Case Western).**

3. **Pre-Vent + neurodevelopmental/DTI follow-up:** Pre-Vent has 717 infants with the most sophisticated continuous monitoring data ever collected. These children are now approximately 5–8 years old — an ideal window for DTI scanning and standardized language testing (CELF-5, PLS-5). Adding a follow-up wave to Pre-Vent would create the definitive dataset. **Contact: Namasivayam Ambalavanan (Alabama, PI), Debra Weese-Mayer (Northwestern), J. Randall Moorman (Virginia).**

### Tier 2: Prospective Design Required

4. **VIBeS/Melbourne + SpO₂ protocol:** VIBeS already has world-class DTI + language follow-up infrastructure. Adding systematic continuous SpO₂ recording to a future VIBeS cohort would complete the triad prospectively. **Contact: Peter Anderson, Jeanie Cheong, Lex Doyle (Melbourne/MCRI).**

### Tier 3: Historical Cohorts with Gaps

5. **ELGAN** — has DTI (subset) and language but no SpO₂. Too old for prospective SpO₂ addition.
6. **EPICure** — similar to ELGAN. Rich outcomes, no SpO₂.
