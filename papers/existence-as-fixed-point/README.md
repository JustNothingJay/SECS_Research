## Summary

Three companion papers establish a closed triangulation: a formal algebra of collapse-based computation [1], a proof that natural systems satisfy the algebra's preconditions because death provides the exhaustive veto partition [2], and a structural connection between the algebra's fixed-point distinction and seven open mathematical frontiers [3]. This paper presents the meta-theory — the claim that emerges when the three results are read together.

The claim is existential: existence itself is the fixed point. Not a property of existence, not something existence has — existence *is* the collapse to purity, the return to the ground state, the structure that persists when everything else is extinguished.

The paper makes the claim precise in five moves:

1. **Sign inversion** — zero is not a boundary between two symmetric domains; zero IS existence. Consciousness is the execution phase of the fixed point, resolving wave/particle duality as the constraint window opening (wave) and closing (particle) with each act of observation.
2. **Biological arc** — from conception (the biological Big Bang) to death (the exhaustive veto), the algebra runs unbroken at three simultaneous scales: constraints, individual, and collective.
3. **Structural elimination** — mirror universes, dual planes, reversible time, and hidden dimensions are each shown to be unnecessary.
4. **Lexicon collapse** — mathematics, physics, biology, philosophy, and theology have described the same structural reality using different vocabularies that collapse to a single formal structure.
5. **Finite memory** — the Bekenstein bound proves the substrate has finite capacity; the pruning chain manages it at every scale from synapses to black holes; the second law is the cost of the bookkeeping; maximum entropy is purity.
