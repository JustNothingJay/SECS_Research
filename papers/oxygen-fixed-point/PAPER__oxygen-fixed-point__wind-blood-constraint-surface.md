## Abstract {-}

Molecular oxygen is maintained at a fixed-point concentration at every scale where chemistry operates: 98% arterial saturation in blood, 20.946% in the troposphere, and approximately 1% of all baryonic mass in the universe. At each scale, the same mechanism operates: a gradient-driven transport system carries oxygen across a selective boundary from a source to a sink, and negative feedback holds the concentration at a unique equilibrium $\omega_0$. The architecture is identical from haemoglobin to wind to stellar convection. Only the scale changes.

This paper presents the oxygen fixed point as a cross-scale instantiation of the Banach contraction mapping, extending the framework established in the companion papers on collapse algebra, osmotic selectivity, and gestational oxygen timing. We show that:

1. The fine structure constant $\alpha$ enters the atmospheric oxygen chain through the Chapman cycle: photodissociation cross-sections scale as $\sigma \propto \alpha^{-4}$, making the ozone column an $\alpha$-dependent boundary. A 1% change in $\alpha$ shifts ozone thickness by approximately 4%.

2. The Brewer-Dobson circulation is structurally isomorphic to cardiovascular circulation: both are gradient-driven transport systems that carry oxygen cargo against a concentration gradient from source to sink.

3. The Great Oxidation Event (2.4 Ga) represents a discrete fixed-point transition --- from anoxic ($< 10^{-5}$%) to the current 20.946% --- driven by biological innovation (cyanobacteria), buffered by geological sinks (banded iron formations), and irreversible once the new $\omega_0$ was established.

4. Iron is the instrument of the oxygen fixed point at every scale: Fe-56 as the kinetic $\omega_0$ of nucleosynthesis, haemoglobin Fe$^{2+}$ as the oxygen carrier, banded iron formations as the geological buffer, and the iron core as the geodynamo that maintains the planetary magnetic membrane.

5. Fire and stellar fusion are the same contraction mapping at chemical and nuclear scales respectively. Photosynthesis is $T^{-1}$ --- the only process that runs the contraction backward.

6. Gravitational collapse (black hole formation) is the gradient-resolution mechanism of last resort: when oxygen accumulates beyond what transport can distribute, the system folds spacetime around the excess.

The forward process (oxidation) and the reverse process (photosynthesis) operate concurrently at every scale. The fixed point exists because the rates are equal at the same moment, not because they alternate.

# The Chain from $\alpha$ to 20.946%

## Chapman Cycle: Where $\alpha$ Enters

The companion paper on osmotic selectivity (Carpenter, 2026b) establishes the chain $\alpha \to a_0 \to$ bond angles $\to$ molecular geometry. That chain extends to atmospheric photochemistry:

$$\alpha \;\to\; \text{molecular absorption cross-sections} \;\to\; \text{Chapman cycle rates} \;\to\; \text{ozone column thickness}$$

The four Chapman reactions (Chapman, 1930) and their $\alpha$-dependence:

| Reaction | $\alpha$-dependence |
|---|---|
| O$_2$ + $h\nu \to$ 2O | $\sigma$(O$_2$) $\propto a_0^2 \propto \alpha^{-4}$: cross-section set by molecular orbital geometry |
| O + O$_2$ + M $\to$ O$_3$ | Pressure-dependent; weakly $\alpha$-dependent through activation barriers |
| O$_3$ + $h\nu \to$ O$_2$ + O | $\sigma$(O$_3$) $\propto \alpha^{-4}$: Hartley band absorption ($\pi \to \pi^*$ at ${\sim}255$ nm) |
| O + O$_3 \to$ 2O$_2$ | Rate constant $k$ weakly $\alpha$-dependent |

The O$_2$ bond dissociation energy is $D_0 = 5.115$ eV, corresponding to a threshold wavelength $\lambda = 242.3$ nm. This energy traces to hydrogenic orbital energies: $D_0 \propto R_\infty \cdot \alpha^2$, where $R_\infty$ is the Rydberg constant. The Hartley band peak cross-section is $\sigma$(O$_3$) $\approx 1.5 \times 10^{-17}$ cm$^2$ at 255 nm (Sander et al., 2011).

**Sensitivity to $\alpha$.** A 1% change in $\alpha$ produces a 4% change in photodissociation cross-sections (since $\sigma \propto \alpha^{-4}$), yielding approximately 4% change in steady-state ozone column --- a loss of approximately 12 Dobson Units from the 300 DU baseline. A 2% change in $\alpha$ would exceed the historical variability of the ozone column from natural cycles. The ozone column is exquisitely sensitive to $\alpha$ through photochemistry.

$$\alpha \to a_0 \to \sigma_{\text{O}_2} \to k_{\text{Chapman}} \to N_{\text{O}_3} \to I_{\text{UV}} \to \text{O}_2 \;\text{fixed point}$$

## Brewer-Dobson Circulation: The Planetary Bloodstream

The Brewer-Dobson circulation (Brewer, 1949; Butchart, 2014) transports ozone from the tropical stratosphere --- where it is produced by high UV flux --- to the poles, where UV flux is low and no local production occurs. The result: ozone is 2--3$\times$ more abundant over the poles than over the tropics, despite all production occurring in the tropics. This is active transport against a gradient, not passive diffusion.

# Fixed-Point Transitions: The Great Oxidation Event

The atmosphere has undergone three discrete fixed-point transitions:

$$\text{Anoxic} \; ({<}10^{-5}\%) \xrightarrow{2.4 \;\text{Ga}} \text{Proterozoic} \; (2\text{--}4\%) \xrightarrow{540 \;\text{Ma}} \text{Phanerozoic} \; (20.946\%)$$

### Transition 1: Anoxic to Proterozoic (2.4 Ga)

Cyanobacteria evolved oxygenic photosynthesis at approximately 2.7 Ga (Lyons et al., 2014). For the next 300 million years, all produced O$_2$ was absorbed by dissolved Fe$^{2+}$ in the oceans, precipitating as banded iron formations (BIFs) --- alternating layers of iron oxide (Fe$_2$O$_3$) and chert (SiO$_2$) that constitute the fossilised record of iron--oxygen oscillations (Holland, 2006; Bekker et al., 2004).

When the Fe$^{2+}$ sink was exhausted (approximately 2.4 Ga), O$_2$ accumulated in the atmosphere for the first time. The consequence was the largest mass extinction in Earth's history: anaerobic organisms, for which oxygen was a lethal toxin, were eliminated. The Huronian glaciation followed, as O$_2$ destroyed the methane greenhouse. The new fixed point --- approximately 2--4% atmospheric O$_2$ --- was stable for approximately two billion years.

**Iron literally mopped up oxygen until it could not anymore. Then the fixed point shifted.**

### Transition 2: Proterozoic to Phanerozoic (540 Ma)

Vascular land plants colonised continents beginning at approximately 460 Ma, massively increasing photosynthetic biomass. Remaining geological reducing agents were progressively depleted. Atmospheric O$_2$ may have peaked at approximately 35% during the Carboniferous (enabling giant insects whose tracheal respiration scales with ambient $pO_2$; Harrison, Kaiser & VandenBrooks, 2010). The system settled at 20.946% --- the current fixed point, set by the Le Chatelier balance between photosynthetic production and aerobic consumption.

### Irreversibility

Each transition is irreversible. The new organisms *depend on* the O$_2$ they produce, preventing reversion. Reverting to the anoxic state would require eliminating all photosynthesis, which is inconsistent with the thermodynamic stability of the oxygen-producing biosphere. The fixed point generates the conditions for its own persistence --- the algebraic structure identified in the collapse algebra (Carpenter, 2026c): $\omega_0$ is held in place by its own existence.

### What Sets the Fixed Point

The biological community. Anaerobes enforced anoxia. Cyanobacteria broke the iron buffer. Land plants overwhelmed the geological sinks. **The fixed point is biological, operating within physical ($\alpha$-determined) constraints.** Change the biology, change $\omega_0$. The algebra predicts *a* fixed point, not *this* fixed point.

## Fe-56: The Kinetic $\omega_0$

A critical subtlety: Fe-56 is not the nucleus with the highest binding energy per nucleon. $${}^{28}\text{Si} \xrightarrow{+\alpha} {}^{32}\text{S} \xrightarrow{+\alpha} \cdots \xrightarrow{+\alpha} {}^{56}\text{Ni} \xrightarrow{\beta^+} {}^{56}\text{Co} \xrightarrow{\beta^+} {}^{56}\text{Fe}$$

The chain terminates at Ni-56 because photodisintegration at $T \gtrsim 3.5$ GK prevents further alpha capture. Ni-56 decays to Fe-56 with $t_{1/2} = 6.075$ d + 77.236 d (Dyson, 1979). Fe-56 is where the reaction network converges --- the kinetic fixed point, not the thermodynamic minimum. The thermodynamic minimum (Ni-62) is reached only on timescales of $\sim 10^{1500}$ years via quantum tunnelling.

**This distinction matters for the thesis.** $\omega_0$ is defined not as the energy minimum but as the convergence point of the contraction mapping --- exactly what Fe-56 is.

# The Black Hole as $\omega_0$

## Photodisintegration: The Reversal

At $T \sim 8$--$10 \times 10^9$ K (0.7--0.9 MeV), core density $\sim 3 \times 10^9$ g/cm$^3$, the iron core undergoes photodisintegration:

$${}^{56}\text{Fe} + \gamma \to 13\,{}^{4}\text{He} + 4n$$

Energy cost: 124.4 MeV per nucleus, absorbing $\sim 1.7 \times 10^{51}$ erg from the core. The binding energy accumulated through $10^7$ years of stellar nucleosynthesis is released back into alpha particles in milliseconds.

Photodisintegration is $T^{-1}$ at the nuclear scale. The forward journey (H $\to$ Fe) took $10^7$ years. The return (Fe $\to$ $\alpha$ $\to$ nuclear matter $\to$ singularity) takes approximately 0.1 seconds. The ratio is approximately $3 \times 10^{15}$.
